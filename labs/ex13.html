Exercise 13: Capsular Staining

Anthony Method - Core Concepts

Key Concepts for the Test

What is a Capsule?

  • Capsule: Distinct, gelatinous layer outside the cell wall
  • Slime layer: Diffuse, irregular layer (less organized)
  • Glycocalyx: Term for polysaccharide capsules ("sugar shell")
  • Exception: Bacillus anthracis has a protein capsule, not polysaccharide

Functions of Capsules (Virulence Factors)

Function Example
Anti-phagocytic - Prevents engulfment by WBCs Streptococcus pneumoniae (pneumonia)
Attachment - Helps adhere to surfaces Streptococcus mutans (dental caries)
Biofilm formation Dental plaque

Anthony Method - Critical Points

DO NOT HEAT-FIX! Heat destroys or shrinks the capsule.
  • Air-dry only - No heat fixation
  • Primary stain: Crystal violet (2 min)
  • Wash with: 20% copper sulfate (NOT water)
  • Copper sulfate acts as both decolorizer and counterstain

Expected Results

Structure Color
Background Purple (crystal violet + milk proteins)
Cell body Dark purple
Capsule Clear or light blue halo

Test-Taking Points

  • Capsules = major virulence factor
  • S. pneumoniae capsule prevents phagocytosis
  • S. mutans capsule causes dental plaque/caries
  • Anthony method: NO heat-fix, use copper sulfate wash
  • Capsule appears as clear halo around dark purple cells
  • Example organism: Klebsiella pneumoniae (has prominent capsule)
