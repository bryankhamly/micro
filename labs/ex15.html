Exercise 15: Endospore Staining

Schaeffer-Fulton Method - Core Concepts

Key Concepts for the Test

What are Endospores?

  • Dormant structures produced when nutrients are depleted
  • Only produced by Bacillus and Clostridium genera
  • Extremely resistant to heat, radiation, acids, chemicals, and desiccation
  • Very dehydrated (10-30% water content)
  • Contain calcium dipicolinate (NOT found in vegetative cells)

Pathogenic Endospore-Forming Bacteria

Organism Disease
Bacillus anthracis Anthrax
Clostridium tetani Tetanus
Clostridium botulinum Botulism
Clostridioides difficile Diarrhea/Colitis (15-30% recurrence due to spore persistence)

Schaeffer-Fulton Method

  • Primary stain: Malachite green (with heat/steam for 7 min)
  • Counterstain: Safranin
  • Heat is required to drive stain into the resistant endospore
Structure Color
Endospores GREEN
Vegetative cells RED/PINK

Alternative: Dorner Method

  • Uses carbolfuchsin (heated) and nigrosin
Structure Color
Endospores RED
Vegetative cells COLORLESS
Background DARK (nigrosin)

Test-Taking Points

  • Only Bacillus and Clostridium make true endospores
  • Endospores = GREEN (Schaeffer-Fulton)
  • Vegetative cells = RED/PINK (Schaeffer-Fulton)
  • Heat is required to drive malachite green into endospores
  • Autoclaving (121Â°C under pressure) kills endospores; boiling does NOT
  • Endospore persistence explains C. diff recurrence (15-30%)
  • Calcium dipicolinate is unique to endospores
  • Endospores form when nutrients are depleted; germinate when nutrients return
