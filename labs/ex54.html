Key Concepts for the Test

Strep vs Staph - Quick Differentiation

Feature Streptococci Staphylococci
Arrangement Chains (or pairs) Clusters (grape-like)
Catalase Test NEGATIVE (no bubbles) POSITIVE (bubbles)
Catalase test is the quickest way to differentiate strep from staph!

S. pneumoniae vs Viridans Strep

Both are alpha-hemolytic (green zone). How to differentiate:

Feature S. pneumoniae Viridans strep
Optochin SENSITIVE Resistant
Bile solubility Positive (dissolves) Negative
Diseases Pneumonia, meningitis, otitis media Dental caries, endocarditis

Test-Taking Points

  • Catalase negative = Strep (not Staph)
  • Beta hemolysis + Bacitracin sensitive = S. pyogenes (Group A)
  • Beta hemolysis + CAMP positive = S. agalactiae (Group B)
  • Bile esculin positive = Group D
  • Bile esculin + 6.5% NaCl tolerant = Enterococcus
  • Alpha hemolysis + Optochin sensitive = S. pneumoniae
  • S. bovis isolation from blood = screen for colon cancer!
  • Read hemolysis in stab areas (anaerobic = clearer pattern)
